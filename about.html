About Me

I am Dr. Nitin Lohan, an Assistant Professor in Mathematics and a researcher in Atmospheric Science, Applied Mathematics and Climate Analytics.

My academic journey includes a PhD in Applied Mathematics from Gautam Buddha University, where my research focused on diagnostic and numerical modelling studies of heavy precipitation events over the Indian region.

I actively work on high-impact weather systems, cloudburst events, monsoon depressions, climate variability, machine learning applications in meteorology and advanced numerical weather prediction systems.

Research Interests

  • Extreme and high-impact weather events
  • Cloudburst diagnostics over Himalayan region
  • Monsoon low pressure systems and heavy rainfall
  • Climate change and rainfall trend analysis
  • Numerical Weather Prediction (WRF / WRFDA)
  • Machine Learning / Deep Learning in Atmospheric Science
  • Remote Sensing and GIS Applications
  • Big Data Analytics in Weather Forecasting
